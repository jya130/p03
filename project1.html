Citymapper Feature Design


While Citymapper strives for providing convenience in exploring the city through transits, the proposed weather feature allows users to experience their journey in a safe and aware way.
stage[1]

Problem Space Research

The first stage of the design involves understanding Citymapper's current interface and the current user journey map by researching both novice users and experienced users. The team then come up with a persona to represent interviewees' mental model, which is to be more aware of unexpected changes and receive alternatives suggestions while travelling.

stage[2]

Brainstorming and Wireframing

The team came up with three scenarios and three coordinating features. Since the idea of sheltered route and weather alert was initiated by me, I was in charge of making the wire flow of the feature. Adobe XD is used in the process to design a set of medium-fidelity wireframes with simple animations to propose to the instructors.

stage[3]

Mockup and Pitch

After deciding on pursuing a weather alert feature, the team separated to do mockups for 1) Feature Onboarding 2) Selecting Sheltered Route 3) Viewing Weather Forecasting Information 4) Set Notifications 5) iPhone Widgets.

I was in charge of making mockups for Selecting Sheltered Route and Viewing Weather Forecasting Information. After making some low-fidelity wireframes in Adobe XD, I exported the framework to AI and use screenshots of Citymapper's current interface as a reference to redesign the interface. Because of the additional information, the hierarchy of the interface was always the biggest concern of the process. The team decided to implement a toogle function to allow users to have the freedom to change the Hierarchy of the information.


self-reflection

After this feature design project, one of the most important take away is the ability to define and identify users' mental models and journey maps. Since the project asks the team to design new featuers, but not to redesign usablities, the team was struggling with how to implement new features while still following the same art direction of Citymapper.

Also, while designing the interface, designers need to keep the visual hierarchy in mind so that the additional elements won't take over the main features of the application, but still give user interactive clues to trigger the feature. Low-fidelity wireframing and wireflowing allow the team to rapidly develop ideas and solutions towards the layout.
